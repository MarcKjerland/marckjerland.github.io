About Me

I am a computational scientist specializing in mathematical modeling, high-performance computing, geophysical applications, and data analysis. Recently I have worked with researchers at the Institute for Environmental Science and Policy at the University of Illinois at Chicago developing novel performance metrics for urban institutions.

From 2015-2017 I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at the Maritime/Coastal Disaster Section of the Disaster Prevention Research Institute at Kyoto University, developing models to simulate coastal flooding caused by typhoons using adaptive mesh refinement.
